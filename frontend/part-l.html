Part L — Energy Efficiency

←

Overview

Energy efficiency regulations including insulation, building fabric, heating systems, and renewable energy integration.

Clauses

Clause 1: Energy efficiency standards

Residential buildings must meet minimum energy efficiency targets, including limits on carbon emissions and fabric performance.

Typical evidence: SAP calculations, EPC reports, fabric U-value schedules.

Clause 2: Fabric performance

Walls, roofs, floors, windows and doors must achieve required U-values to reduce heat loss.

Typical evidence: construction details, insulation specifications, thermal bridging calculations.

Clause 3: Heating and Hot water systems

Heating, hot water systems must be energy efficient and appropriately controlled.

Typical evidence: system specifications, commissioning certificates, control strategies.

Clause 4: Low carbon and renewable technologies

Where required, low carbon or renewable technologies should be used to help meet carbon reduction targets.

Typical evidence: PV layouts, heat pump specifications, energy strategy reports.

Energy Checklist

  • Check insulation levels
  • Verify heating system efficiency
  • Review renewable energy options
  • Ensure energy performance documentation
