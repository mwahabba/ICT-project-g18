Part F — Ventilation

←

Overview

Ventilation requirements for residential buildings, including natural and mechanical ventilation, indoor air quality, and compliance guidance.

Clauses

Clause 1: Minimum ventilation rates

Residential buildings must provide minimum ventilation rates to ensure adequate indoor air quality for occupants under normal use.

Typical evidence: ventilation calculations, design specifications, compliance reports.

Clause 2: Mechanical Systems Requirements

Mechanical ventilation systems must be correctly designed, installed, and commissioned to meet airflow and performance requirements.

Typical evidence: commissioning certificates, system layouts, manufacturer data.

Clause 3: Natural ventilation standards

Where natural ventilation is used, openings must be sufficient in size and location to provide effective air movement.

Typical evidence: window schedules, opening area calculations, drawings.

Clause 4: Indoor air quality measures

Measures must be taken to limit the buildup of moisture, pollutants, and condensation to protect occupant health.

Typical evidence: IAQ assessments, humidity calculations, maintenance guidance.

Ventilation Checklist

  • Inspect ventilation system
  • Check compliance with air quality standards
  • Verify mechanical and natural ventilation rates
